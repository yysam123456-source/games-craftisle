??? Building Tips

Plan Before You Build

It's tempting to start placing tiles immediately, but spend 30 seconds thinking about your layout first. Decide where your shoreline will be, where the highest point is, and where your main focal point (church, windmill, etc.) will sit.

Use Layers for Depth

Place taller objects (trees, windmills) behind shorter ones (houses, paths) to create a sense of depth. This simple technique makes flat 2D scenes look much more dimensional.

Leave Breathing Room

Don't fill every single tile. Empty space ˇŞ especially grass and sand ˇŞ makes your island feel more natural and less cluttered. Think of it as "visual breathing room."

Color Coordination

Stick to a consistent color palette. For authentic Mediterranean looks, use:

  • White for buildings
  • Blue for domes and accents
  • Earth tones (sand, terracotta) for ground
  • Greens for nature

?? Pro Techniques

The Rule of Thirds

Place your most important building (church, windmill) at one of the intersection points of an imaginary 3ˇÁ3 grid over your island. This creates a more visually pleasing composition than centering everything.

Create Natural Shorelines

Avoid perfectly circular or square islands. Use irregular edges, inlets, and peninsulas. Mix water depths by placing lighter blue tiles near the shore and darker blues further out.

Tell a Story

The best islands feel like real places. Add details that suggest life: a boat at the dock, a path leading to a door, a garden that looks tended. These narrative details make viewers imagine the people who live there.
