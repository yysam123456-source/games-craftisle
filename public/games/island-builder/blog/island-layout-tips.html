10 Island Layout Tips for Beginners

Published May 21, 2026 ˇ¤ 5 min read

Building your first island can feel overwhelming. With 14ˇÁ14 grid cells and 75+ assets to choose from, where do you start? These 10 tips will help you create beautiful, balanced islands from day one.

1. Start with Terrain, Not Buildings

Before placing a single house, lay down your terrain. Use Grass as your base, then carve out paths with Path tiles. Add Sand along the edges for beaches, and Water for canals. Terrain gives your island structure ˇŞ buildings are decoration on top.

2. The One-Third Water Rule

Every great Mykonos Island uses water. But how much? The sweet spot is 2-3 rows of water ˇŞ about 15-20% of your grid. Too much water and you lose building space; too little and the island feeling disappears. Place water along one or two edges, not scattered randomly.

3. Paths Create Flow

A simple cross-shaped path (one horizontal, one vertical) instantly organizes your island into quadrants. Each quadrant can have its own character: a village in one, a chapel district in another, gardens in a third. Paths also make your island feel lived-in and intentional.

4. Cluster Buildings by Type

Don't scatter buildings randomly. Group similar structures together: houses with houses, churches with altars and lanterns, windmills on hills. Clusters create natural "districts" that make your island feel like a real village rather than a random collection.

5. The Chapel on the Hill

Place your Main Chapel or Tower Chapel on higher ground (if using stairs) or at least in a prominent position. Surround it with lanterns, stone walls, and cypress trees. This creates a focal point that draws the eye ˇŞ every Mykonos village has its church as the centerpiece.

6. Nature Breaks the Grid

Buildings are rigid; nature is organic. Use Cypress trees, Bougainvillea, Agave, and Olive trees to soften the edges of your village. Place them between buildings, along paths, and in irregular clusters. Nature makes your island feel alive, not just constructed.

7. Props Add Life

A house is just a house ˇŞ but add a Bench, Lantern Post, Terracotta Pot, and a Crate nearby, and suddenly someone lives there. Small props are the difference between a model and a world. Spend time on the details.

8. Leave Empty Space

New builders tend to fill every cell. Don't. Leave gaps between buildings, open plazas, empty grass patches. Negative space makes your placed elements stand out. A cluttered island is hard to read; a spacious one feels serene.

9. The Edge Effect

The border of your island is prime real estate. Place a Sea Wall along the water's edge. Add a Small Bridge over your canal. Put palm-like Agave plants on the beach. The edges define the island's silhouette ˇŞ make them count.

10. Iterate, Don't Perfect

Your first layout won't be your last. Use the Erase tool (right-click) freely. Try placing a building, stepping back, and deciding if it fits. The best islands are built through experimentation, not planning. Start placing, and let the island reveal itself.
