# GTA Simulator

**Category:** Action  
**Difficulty:** Medium

---

## Description

GTA Simulator brings the open-world chaos of Grand Theft Auto to your browser! Explore a sprawling city, steal cars, complete missions, and cause mayhem. With a simplified control scheme optimized for web play, it captures the essence of the classic GTA experience.

---

## How to Play GTA Simulator

1. **Start in the City** – You begin on a street in a pixel-art city. Familiarize yourself with the controls.

2. **Steal a Car** – Walk up to any parked car and press the action button to enter and drive it.

3. **Complete Missions** – Look for mission markers (usually colored icons on the map). Walk into them to start missions.

4. **Drive Anywhere** – The city is open. Explore at your own pace. Drive on roads, off-road, or into buildings (carefully!).

5. **Avoid Police** – If you commit crimes (hit pedestrians, crash into cars, shoot), police will chase you. Lose them by escaping their line of sight.

6. **Use Weapons** – Pick up weapons from the ground or buy them. Shoot with the fire button.

7. **Save Your Progress** – Find a safe house to save your game.

## Tips & Strategies

- **Master the Driving Physics** – Cars handle differently. Sports cars are fast but slippery; SUVs are stable but slow.
- **Watch Your Wanted Level** – Stars at the top of the screen indicate police attention. More stars = more aggressive police.
- **Use Alleys to Escape** – When chased, duck into narrow alleys where police cars can't follow.
- **Complete Side Missions** – Taxi, ambulance, and police side missions give bonuses and unlockables.
- **Save Often** – The game can be unpredictable. Save before attempting risky missions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this the real GTA?**
A: No – this is a fan-made browser game inspired by GTA. It's not affiliated with Rockstar Games.

**Q: Can I play multiplayer?**
A: The web version is single-player only.

**Q: How big is the map?**
A: The city is substantial with multiple districts: downtown, industrial, residential, and countryside.
