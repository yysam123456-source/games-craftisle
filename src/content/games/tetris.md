# Tetris - Complete Game Guide

## 🎮 Game Overview

Tetris is THE most iconic puzzle game in history. Created by Soviet software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in 1984, it has sold over 500 million copies across all platforms, making it one of the best-selling games of all time. The premise is simple: falling blocks (Tetrominoes) that you rotate and place to complete lines. But the execution is pure genius—combining spatial reasoning, quick reflexes, and strategic planning into an endlessly replayable experience.

## 📜 History & Cultural Impact

- **June 6, 1984**: Alexey Pajitnov creates Tetris on an Elektronika 60 computer in Moscow
- **1989**: Nintendo bundles Tetris with the Game Boy, cementing its place in gaming history
- **2007**: Listed by Game Informer as #1 "Most Important Video Game of All Time"
- **2009**: Recognized by Guinness World Records as the "Most Ported Game"
- **2014**: Tetris turns 30 (celebrated with a Google Doodle)
- **2023**: Tetris Effect: Connected wins multiple Game of the Year awards

## 🎯 How to Play

### Basic Rules
1. **Tetrominoes**: Seven differently-shaped blocks (I, O, T, S, Z, J, L)
2. **Falling**: Blocks fall from the top of the screen
3. **Movement**: Move left/right, rotate, and speed up descent
4. **Clearing Lines**: Complete a horizontal line to clear it and score points
5. **Game Over**: Stack blocks to the top of the playfield

### Controls
- **Move Left/Right**: Arrow keys or A/D
- **Rotate**: Up arrow, X, or Space
- **Soft Drop**: Down arrow or S (faster fall)
- **Hard Drop**: Space or Up arrow (instant fall)
- **Hold Piece**: C or Shift (save a piece for later)

### Scoring
- **Single**: 100 points x level
- **Double**: 300 points x level
- **Triple**: 500 points x level
- **Tetris (4 lines)**: 800 points x level
- **T-Spin**: Bonus points for spinning T piece into tight spaces
- **Back-to-Back**: Bonus for consecutive Tetrises or T-Spins

## 🧠 Strategy Guide

### Beginner Tips
1. **Flat Surface**: Keep the stack as flat as possible
2. **Don't Leave Holes**: Gaps are your enemy—they waste space
3. **Save the I-Tetromino**: The long piece (I) is best for clearing 4 lines at once
4. **Use the Hold Piece**: Store a useful piece for later

### Advanced Strategies

#### The T-Spin Technique
- Rotate the T piece into a tight space (requires skill and precision)
- Awards massive bonus points
- Essential for high-level play

#### 6-3 or 7-3 Stacking
- Build a "well" on one side of the grid
- Perfect for setting up back-to-back Tetrises
- Used by competitive players

#### Perfect Clear
- Clear ALL blocks from the grid (extremely rare)
- Awards 10x bonus points
- The ultimate Tetris achievement

### Common Mistakes
- Creating holes in the stack
- Rotating pieces unnecessarily (wastes time)
- Not planning for the "next" piece
- Using the I-piece too early

## 🎲 Game Modes

- **Marathon**: Classic mode, clear lines to advance levels
- **Sprint**: Clear 40 lines as fast as possible
- **Ultra**: Score as many points as possible in 2 minutes
- **Battle**: Versus mode against other players
- **Tetris Effect**: Enhanced visual and audio experience with VR support
