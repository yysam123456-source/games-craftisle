# Galaga

**Category:** Arcade  
**Difficulty:** Medium

---

## Description

Galaga is a legendary fixed-shooter arcade game from 1981. Pilot your starship, destroy waves of alien Galaga forces, and rescue captured teammates. With its iconic enemy formations, challenging boss stages, and the famous 'dual fighter' power-up, Galaga remains one of the most beloved shoot-em-ups of all time.

---

## How to Play Galaga

1. **Move Your Ship** – Use arrow keys (← →) or touch to move your ship horizontally at the bottom of the screen.

2. **Fire Missiles** – Press Space or tap to shoot. You can hold the button for rapid fire in some versions.

3. **Dodge Enemy Attacks** – Aliens fly in formation and periodically break off to attack. They shoot projectiles or dive-bomb your ship.

4. **Destroy All Enemies** – Clear the wave to advance to the next stage. Each wave has a specific formation pattern.

5. **Watch for Boss Galaga** – At the end of certain waves, a Boss Galaga appears. Defeat it for bonus points.

6. **Avoid Capture** – The Tractor Beam enemy can capture your ship. If this happens, destroy the capturing enemy to rescue your ship and form a Dual Fighter (double firepower!).

7. **Survive as Long as Possible** – The game gets faster and more difficult with each wave. How far can you go?

## Tips & Strategies

- **Memorize Enemy Patterns** – Each wave type has a specific attack pattern. Learning them is key to survival.
- **Don't Wasting Shots** – Lead your shots. Aim where the enemies will be, not where they are.
- **Prioritize Tractor Beam Enemies** – Deal with them quickly to avoid losing your ship.
- **Use the Sides** – Enemies often attack from the center. Staying near the edges can be safer.
- **Rescue Your Ship ASAP** – A Dual Fighter (after rescue) is much more powerful. Prioritize the rescue mission.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What's the maximum score?**
A: The original arcade version rolls over at 999,990 points, but modern versions may not have this limit.

**Q: How many stages are there?**
A: The game has 255 stages (a kill screen at stage 256 in the original).

**Q: What does 'Dual Fighter' do?**
A: After rescuing your captured ship, you control two ships simultaneously with double firepower.
