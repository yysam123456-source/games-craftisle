# 2048 - Complete Game Guide

## 🎮 Game Overview

2048 is a deceptively simple yet deeply addictive sliding puzzle game created by Italian developer Gabriele Cirulli in March 2014. What started as a weekend project quickly became a global phenomenon, reaching over 10 million players in just two weeks. The game's genius lies in its elegant simplicity: slide numbered tiles on a 4x4 grid, merge matching numbers, and try to create the ultimate 2048 tile.

## 📜 History & Cultural Impact

- **March 16, 2014**: Gabriele Cirulli publishes 2048 as an open-source project
- **Viral Spread**: The game spread through social media, workplaces, and classrooms worldwide
- **Critical Reception**: Praised for its "just one more try" addictive quality
- **Legacy**: Inspired hundreds of variants (1024, 4096, 8192, Fibonacci, and more)
- **Platform**: Originally web-based, now available on iOS, Android, and virtually every platform

## 🎯 How to Play

### Basic Rules
1. **Grid**: 4x4 grid with numbered tiles
2. **Starting Tiles**: Two tiles (either 2 or 4) appear randomly on the grid
3. **Movement**: Slide all tiles in one of four directions (up, down, left, right)
4. **Merging**: When two tiles with the same number collide, they merge into one tile with their sum
5. **Goal**: Create a tile with the number 2048

### Controls
- **Keyboard**: Arrow keys or WASD
- **Touch/Mobile**: Swipe in any direction
- **Mouse**: Click and drag (some versions)

### Scoring
- Each merge adds the value of the new tile to your score
- Example: Merging two 1024 tiles creates a 2048 tile and adds 2048 points to your score

## 🧠 Strategy Guide

### Beginner Tips
1. **Pick a Corner**: Always keep your highest tile in one corner (usually bottom-right)
2. **Build Around It**: Arrange tiles so smaller numbers are away from your corner
3. **Avoid the Wrong Direction**: Never move tiles away from your chosen corner
4. **Keep It Organized**: Try to maintain descending order from your corner

### Advanced Strategies

#### The Corner Strategy (Recommended for Beginners)
1. Choose a corner (e.g., bottom-right)
2. Always move tiles TOWARD that corner
3. Keep your highest tile pinned in the corner
4. Build a snake pattern: highest to lowest radiating out from corner

#### Tile Management
- **Priority**: Keep high tiles together, separate low tiles
- **Avoid Isolation**: Don't let a high tile get surrounded by low tiles
- **Plan Ahead**: Always think 3-4 moves ahead

### Common Mistakes
- Moving tiles away from your corner/edge
- Letting high tiles get separated
- Filling the grid randomly without strategy
- Panicking when the grid gets full
